NASA's Mars Perseverance rover searches for signs of ancient microbial life and collects samples for possible Earth return. Launched July 30, 2020 and landed Feb 18, 2021, the rover targets Jezero Crater and has objectives: study Mars' habitability, seek signs of past microbial life, collect and cache samples, and prepare for future human missions. Recent updates report potential biosignatures found in a sample called 'Sapphire Canyon' and continuing science operations.
